GROUNDING, BREATHING, AND IMMEDIATE COPING TECHNIQUES
Source: Verified mindfulness and evidence-based coping resources

--- GROUNDING TECHNIQUES ---

PURPOSE: Grounding techniques interrupt the anxiety or distress spiral by bringing your attention firmly into the present moment — the here and now. They are particularly useful during panic attacks, dissociative moments, or when the mind is flooded with overwhelming thoughts.

1. THE 5-4-3-2-1 TECHNIQUE (Sensory Grounding)
Name out loud or mentally:
→ 5 things you can SEE right now (a poster, your hand, the ceiling fan, a water bottle, your phone)
→ 4 things you can physically TOUCH and describe the sensation (rough desk, soft shirt, cool metal, smooth glass)
→ 3 things you can HEAR (AC hum, birds, keyboard sounds)
→ 2 things you can SMELL (or would like to smell)
→ 1 thing you can TASTE
This technique works because it forces the brain's attention onto sensory data, overriding the anxiety narrative.

2. COLD WATER GROUNDING
Run cold water over your wrists, or hold an ice cube. The sudden sensory input interrupts the stress response and signals safety to the nervous system. Particularly effective for moments of acute distress.

3. FEET ON THE FLOOR
Press your feet firmly into the floor. Notice the sensation of contact — the weight, the texture. This physical anchoring is used in trauma therapy to re-establish embodied presence.

4. OBJECT FOCUS
Pick up any object near you. Describe it in as much detail as possible — its weight, colour, texture, edges, temperature. This is a thought-interruption technique.

--- BREATHING TECHNIQUES ---

5. BOX BREATHING (4-4-4-4) — Military and Clinical Standard
Used by Navy SEALs and in clinical anxiety treatment:
→ Inhale for 4 counts
→ Hold for 4 counts
→ Exhale for 4 counts
→ Hold empty for 4 counts
Repeat 4-6 cycles. This regulates the autonomic nervous system.

6. 4-7-8 BREATHING — For Acute Anxiety and Sleep
→ Inhale through nose: 4 counts
→ Hold: 7 counts
→ Exhale through mouth: 8 counts (make a quiet "whoosh" sound)
The extended exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system — your body's "rest and digest" mode.

7. PHYSIOLOGICAL SIGH — Fastest Anxiety Reset
→ Take a normal inhale through nose
→ At the top, add a second short nasal inhale (to fully inflate lungs)
→ Long, slow exhale through mouth
Stanford neuroscientist research shows this is the single fastest way to reduce physiological arousal.

8. DIAPHRAGMATIC BREATHING (Belly Breathing)
Place one hand on chest, one on belly. Breathe so only the belly hand rises. Chest should remain still. Breathing from the diaphragm — not the chest — activates the calming response.

--- BEHAVIOURAL ACTIVATION ---

For low mood and depression, behavioural activation (BA) is one of the most evidence-supported interventions.

PRINCIPLE: When depressed, we withdraw from activities. This withdrawal reduces sources of pleasure and mastery, which deepens depression. BA interrupts this cycle.

HOW TO PRACTICE:
1. List 10 activities you used to enjoy or that gave you a sense of accomplishment
2. Rate them on difficulty (1-5)
3. Schedule the easiest 2-3 into your week as non-negotiable appointments
4. After doing them, record how you felt (even if forced)

Start small. The goal is not to "feel motivated" first — the goal is to act first, and let the feeling follow.

--- JOURNALING PROMPTS FOR DISTRESS ---
- "Right now I am feeling ___ and it makes sense because ___"
- "The thought making me most anxious is ___ and the evidence for/against it is ___"
- "One small thing I can control today is ___"
- "If my best friend felt what I'm feeling, I would tell them ___"
