INDIAN MENTAL HEALTH HELPLINES AND RESOURCES
Source: Verified institutional sources — iCall TISS, Vandrevala Foundation, NIMHANS, iCALL

CRISIS HELPLINES (INDIA):

1. iCALL — TISS (Tata Institute of Social Sciences)
Phone: 9152987821
Website: icallhelpline.org
Hours: Monday–Saturday, 8 AM – 10 PM
Language: English and Hindi
Cost: FREE for students
About: iCall is a psychosocial helpline run by trained counsellors from TISS — one of India's most respected social science institutions. It provides telephone, email, and chat-based counselling. Particularly well-suited for students. iCall also operates a network of on-campus counselling services in Mumbai. Counsellors are culturally grounded, non-judgemental, and trained in evidence-based approaches.

2. Vandrevala Foundation
Phone: 1860-2662-345
Website: vandrevalafoundation.com
Hours: 24/7 — round the clock
Language: English, Hindi, and regional languages
Cost: FREE
About: A 24/7 mental health helpline offering immediate emotional support and crisis intervention. Available for everyone — not just students. Also has a chat option on their website.

3. NIMHANS (National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences)
Location: Bangalore
Phone: 080-46110007
Website: nimhans.ac.in
About: India's premier mental health research and treatment institute. Outpatient services available. Has published extensive public mental health education resources.

4. Snehi
Phone: 044-24640050
About: Provides emotional support and crisis counselling across India. Particularly active in southern states.

5. iCall Chat / Email
Email: icall@tiss.edu
About: If calling feels too difficult, iCall also accepts email counselling requests. Response times vary but counsellors do respond to emails.

WHAT TO EXPECT WHEN YOU CALL:
- You will be greeted by a trained counsellor (not automated)
- You can remain anonymous — you do not have to share your name
- The counsellor will listen, not judge, and not report to anyone
- You can call simply to talk — you do not need to be "in crisis"
- Calls are confidential (with limits for serious risk to life, which the counsellor will explain)

MYTHS ABOUT CALLING A HELPLINE:
Myth: "I'm not bad enough to call."
Truth: You don't need to be at rock bottom. Calling when you are struggling (not yet in crisis) is exactly when a helpline is most helpful.

Myth: "They'll tell my parents or college."
Truth: Calls are confidential. Counsellors only break confidentiality if there is immediate serious risk to life — and they will tell you before doing so.

Myth: "I'll be judged."
Truth: Counsellors are specifically trained to be non-judgemental. They have heard everything. Your experience will not shock or burden them.

IF YOU ARE IN IMMEDIATE DANGER:
Please call 112 (India's unified emergency number) or go to your nearest emergency room.
Do not wait. You matter. Help is available right now.
